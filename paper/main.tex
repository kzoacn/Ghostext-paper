\begin{abstract}
Ghostext is a deterministic, fail-closed linguistic steganography protocol for large language models with authenticated packet framing and implementation-aligned approximation bounds. It uses arithmetic-coding-style interval narrowing over next-token distributions, but the present paper is positioned as a protocol-engineering contribution rather than a near-perfect-security claim. In this revision, the real-backend evidence is limited to a pilot 18-trial template-known baseline under a passive-censor setting; all 18 trials decoded successfully, but the corresponding Wilson 95\% interval $[0.824,1.000]$ makes this only an operational floor for recoverability, runtime, and payload efficiency rather than a settled reliability or detector-evasion result. We explicitly separate measured evidence from open items: the implementation-side TV-style audit diagnostic is uncalibrated against detector scores, natural-tail completion is not claimed as an independent evasion mechanism, detector-facing claims remain pending, and active text modification in transit is out of scope.
\end{abstract}
