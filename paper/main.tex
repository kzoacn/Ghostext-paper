\title{Ghostext: Deterministic and Fail-Closed Text Steganography over Local LLM Token Distributions}

% Anonymous submission: do not include authors in this version.

\renewcommand{\shortauthors}{Anonymous Submission}

\begin{abstract}
We present Ghostext, a security-oriented text steganography system that embeds encrypted payloads into local language-model generation while prioritizing deterministic recoverability and fail-closed behavior under configuration mismatch. The system uses encrypt-then-steganography packet construction, candidate-set policy constraints, retokenization-stability checks, integer quantization, and interval decoding with explicit configuration fingerprint validation. We formalize Ghostext's deployment assumptions and evaluate (i) matched-configuration correctness, (ii) fail-closed behavior under prompt/seed/key/text mismatches, and (iii) practical overhead. We discuss limits including robustness to active text edits and steganalysis resistance, which remain outside the current guarantee scope.
\end{abstract}
