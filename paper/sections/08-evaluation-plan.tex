\section{Evaluation}

Our evaluation targets three questions: (Q1) decode reliability under realistic prompt/message diversity; (Q2) runtime overhead of deterministic safety controls; (Q3) payload efficiency in packet-carrying tokens and in total transmitted tokens. Detector-facing and approximation-instrumentation questions are pending and out of the measured core. The baseline configuration is \texttt{top\_p=0.995}, \texttt{max\_candidates=64}, \texttt{min\_entropy\_bits=0.0}, \texttt{total\_frequency=4096}.

Let $M$ be message count, $S$ successful decodes, $B_i$ packet bytes for sample $i$, $T_i^{\mathrm{pkt}}$ packet-carrying tokens, $T_i^{\mathrm{all}}$ total transmitted tokens. We report $\mathrm{SR}=S/M$, packet-token efficiency $\eta_{\mathrm{pkt}}=\sum_i 8B_i/\sum_i T_i^{\mathrm{pkt}}$, transmission-token efficiency $\eta_{\mathrm{all}}=\sum_i 8B_i/\sum_i T_i^{\mathrm{all}}$, and median/p90 runtime.

\subsection{Same-Backend NLS-Style Comparison Slice}
\label{sec:aligned-slice}

To isolate payload formation from model-family confounders, we compare three methods on the six released Qwen cases using the same backend and candidate policy: (i) Ghostext full; (ii) an implementation-aligned NLS-style single-segment baseline carrying raw UTF-8 plaintext bytes; (iii) the same single-segment baseline carrying Ghostext's AEAD packet bytes. To avoid cross-tokenizer confounding, the single-segment baselines reuse the retokenization-stability filter. This is an implementation-aligned isolation of what payload bitstream is fed into the cover coder, not a historical reproduction of Ziegler et al.

Table~\ref{tab:aligned-nls-slice} supports two narrow conclusions. First, feeding the cover generator with AEAD packet bytes is best read as \emph{cryptographic payload normalization} rather than a claim that a one-bit entropy statistic sharply separates payload sources: raw UTF-8 already has one-bit entropy $0.991$ in this slice, and the AEAD rows are $1.000$. The reason to prefer packet bytes is computational secret-independence under AEAD assumptions, not marginal bit-balance gains. Second, payload normalization alone is not operationally sufficient: the raw-UTF-8 single-segment baseline stalls on the longest English case, and the AEAD single-segment baseline stalls on two of the three English cases. Ghostext full, which carries the same AEAD packet but resolves it through bootstrap/body segmentation, succeeds on all six. At $n=6$ per method the slice is descriptive rather than statistical; a larger-sample aligned table and a direct OD-Stega row remain pending.
