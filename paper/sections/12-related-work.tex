\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-positioning}

NLS~\cite{ziegler2019neural} established arithmetic-coding neural steganography. Its main conceptual move is to map hidden bits onto LM token choices while keeping the generated sequence close to the model distribution. Ghostext inherits that arithmetic-coding viewpoint and the Alice/Bob/Censor communication framing, but it focuses on the systems questions that become acute in a local LLM setting: what exact bitstream is embedded, how prompt and tokenizer state are synchronized, and how replay failure is surfaced when practical candidate truncation and retokenization are involved. Later work explored adaptive and near-imperceptible variants~\cite{shen2020near,dai2019towards,yang2019rnnstega,fang2017generating} and stronger formal constructions~\cite{zhang2021provably,ding2023discop,dewitt2024perfect}. OD-Stega~\cite{huang2026odstega} is most directly related in its constrained-distribution perspective and explicit tokenization-mismatch emphasis, which motivates our retokenization-stability filtering.

Ghostext's novelty is therefore at the systems-integration level. It combines cryptographic payload normalization, authenticated packet framing, and retokenization-aware candidate control in one pipeline. Relative to the formal distribution-preserving line~\cite{zhang2021provably,ding2023discop,dewitt2024perfect}, we use ``near-distribution-preserving'' in a weaker, implementation-aligned sense. Against the same-policy natural-cover baseline, the only residual per-step deviations are integer quantization and retokenization filtering. Classical security frameworks~\cite{cachin1998information,hopper2002provably} remain the conceptual baseline for strong claims, and Ghostext does not claim to replace them.

Two adjacent literatures are also relevant for interpreting our scope. LLM watermarking manipulates generation distributions under detectability constraints~\cite{kirchenbauer2023watermark}; it shares the language of distribution control, but its operational goal is almost the opposite of steganography, since the watermark owner wants later detectability. Detector papers and practical steganalysis pipelines~\cite{joulin2017fasttext,mitchell2023detectgpt} matter because they define what an informed passive observer might actually run, but those results cannot be borrowed without direct experiments on Ghostext outputs. Meteor~\cite{kaptchuk2021language} further illustrates that practical language steganography depends on explicit assumptions about the communication channel and shared model state. Our empirical section is intentionally limited to a fixed prompt-message grid under one pinned backend rather than a historical reproduction of NLS/OD-Stega under their original settings; we therefore do not make broad superiority claims, and whether this systems combination yields significant detector-facing gains remains an open empirical question.
