\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-positioning}

NLS~\cite{ziegler2019neural} established arithmetic-coding neural steganography. Later work explored adaptive and near-imperceptible variants~\cite{shen2020near,dai2019towards,yang2019rnnstega,fang2017generating} and stronger formal constructions~\cite{zhang2021provably,ding2023discop,dewitt2024perfect}. OD-Stega~\cite{huang2026odstega} is most directly related in its constrained-distribution perspective and explicit tokenization-mismatch emphasis, which motivates our retokenization-stability filtering. Foundational information-theoretic and provable-security frameworks~\cite{cachin1998information,hopper2002provably} remain the conceptual baseline for strong security claims; Ghostext does not claim to replace them. LLM watermarking manipulates generation distributions under detectability constraints~\cite{kirchenbauer2023watermark}, and practical detector evaluation combines lightweight baselines with stronger detectors~\cite{joulin2017fasttext,mitchell2023detectgpt}. Meteor~\cite{kaptchuk2021language} further illustrates that practical language steganography depends on explicit assumptions about the communication channel and the shared model.

Ghostext's novelty is at the systems-integration level: combining cryptographic payload normalization, authenticated packet framing, and retokenization-aware candidate control in one deterministic pipeline. Relative to the formal distribution-preserving line~\cite{zhang2021provably,ding2023discop,dewitt2024perfect}, we use ``near-distribution-preserving'' in a weaker, implementation-aligned sense. Against the same-policy natural-cover baseline, the only residual per-step deviations are integer quantization and retokenization filtering. Our current empirical section is intentionally limited to the released end-to-end benchmark rather than a historical reproduction of NLS/OD-Stega under their original settings; we therefore do not make broad superiority claims, and whether this systems combination yields significant detector-facing gains remains an open empirical question.
