\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Classical and Provable Steganography}
The security framing of hidden communication under observation begins with Simmons' prisoners' problem and later formal treatments of steganographic limits and constructions~\cite{DBLP:conf/crypto/Simmons83,DBLP:journals/jsac/AndersonP98,DBLP:conf/crypto/HopperLA02}. Ghostext does not introduce a new information-theoretic definition; instead, it focuses on protocol engineering for deterministic local-LLM recovery under explicit synchronization assumptions.

\subsection{Neural and LLM Text Steganography}
Neural linguistic steganography evolved from LSTM generation methods~\cite{DBLP:conf/acl/FangJA17} to arithmetic-coded neural generation~\cite{ziegler-etal-2019-neural} and later LLM-era variants with stronger formal or algorithmic treatments~\cite{DBLP:conf/acl/ZhangYYH21,DBLP:conf/naacl/LinLZL24,DBLP:journals/tdsc/QiCZZY25}. A practical issue emphasized in recent work is tokenization inconsistency, which can break decode reliability even when the generated text looks fluent~\cite{DBLP:conf/emnlp/YanM25}. Ghostext directly incorporates this concern through a retokenization-stability guard.

\subsection{LLM Watermarking and Distribution Shaping}
We also position Ghostext against LLM watermarking because both areas manipulate token-level distributions and face detectability versus reliability tradeoffs. Prior watermarking work introduced practical generation-time schemes~\cite{conf/icml/KirchenbauerGWK23}, studied reliability boundaries under stronger attack models~\cite{conf/iclr/KirchenbauerGWS24}, and identified fundamental impossibility limits for strong watermarking in language models~\cite{conf/icml/ZhangEF0AB24}. Ghostext has a different objective (covert payload communication rather than origin tracing), but the technical overlap in probability shaping and recoverability constraints is substantial.

\subsection{Security Systems and Covert Communication Deployments}
Security-oriented systems work such as Meteor and Discop emphasizes stronger security formulations and deployment-aware design for realistic distributions~\cite{DBLP:conf/ccs/KaptchukJ0R21,DBLP:conf/sp/DingCWZZY23}. Follow-up work studies practical deployment tradeoffs and extends secure steganography ideas to diffusion settings~\cite{DBLP:conf/codaspy/BauerHMBS24,DBLP:conf/ccs/JoisBK24}. Relative to these lines, Ghostext makes a narrower claim: deterministic recoverability and fail-closed behavior in a local LLM pipeline under strict synchronization.

\subsection{Steganalysis}
Linguistic steganalysis has progressed from feature-based and LM-assisted methods to hybrid frameworks and LLM-assisted detectors~\cite{DBLP:conf/icassp/YiWFZ22,DBLP:journals/spl/GuoLYYZ22,DBLP:conf/icassp/YangYGZGH23,DBLP:journals/corr/abs-2405-09090}. This literature motivates conservative wording in our paper: without dedicated detector experiments, broad undetectability claims are out of scope.
