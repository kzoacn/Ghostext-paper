\section{Ethical Considerations}
This paper studies covert communication and therefore has clear dual-use risk. The same techniques that help privacy-preserving communication under surveillance pressure may also be misused for policy evasion. We manage this risk by keeping claims conservative, specifying a narrow passive-censor scope, and avoiding unsupported statements about universal undetectability or censorship resistance.

Our experimental protocol also avoids unnecessary escalation: we use local controlled environments, toy backends for protocol checks, and limited real-backend demonstrations targeted at recoverability and fail-closed behavior. We do not release any claim as ``secure in general'' without evidence for that exact threat model. This boundary-first reporting style is intended to reduce overclaiming and to support responsible security research practice.
