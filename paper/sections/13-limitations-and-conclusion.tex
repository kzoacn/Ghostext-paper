\section{Limitations and Conclusion}

\paragraph{Limitations.} Ghostext targets passive-text observation and does not provide empirical detector-resistance evidence. The main LLM-backend evidence is now a 100-trial fixed prompt-message grid on one pinned Qwen/\texttt{llama-cpp} stack; although all 100 trials decode successfully, the evidence remains template-known, single-backend, and local-machine specific. Encode latency shows a visible retry-driven tail under low-entropy or unstable-candidate local contexts. Cross-family generalization, direct distribution-distance measurement, and active text modification in transit remain out of scope.

\paragraph{Conclusion.} Ghostext contributes a near-distribution-preserving, implementation-auditable LLM steganography protocol with authenticated packetization and synchronization binding. Relative to the same-policy natural-cover baseline, only two implementation-level effects remain: integer quantization and retokenization filtering. On the released fixed $10 \times 10$ prompt-message grid, the pinned local implementation achieves 100/100 exact recoveries with median encode/decode latencies of $37.74$\,s/$34.47$\,s and mean encode payload density of $2.619$ bits/token. This supports a narrow systems claim: Ghostext can provide deterministic fail-closed round trips under a shared prompt and pinned runtime, while throughput still has a meaningful retry-driven tail. Detector-facing, traffic-side, and cross-family evaluation under the same artifact discipline remains the next milestone.

One practical takeaway from the current artifact is that Ghostext should be read as a protocolized covert channel rather than as a generic prompt trick. The passphrase, prompt, backend metadata, tokenizer behavior, and retry policy are all part of one shared replay contract. That is what makes exact recovery possible, and it is also what bounds the current evidence. In other words, the main contribution is not that a language model can hide bits in principle, but that a concrete local implementation can do so with authenticated framing, auditable failure behavior, and reproducible measurements under a pinned stack.
