\section{Conclusion}

This paper draft establishes an evidence-grounded baseline for the Ghostext CCS effort. The current implementation supports encrypted packet embedding over LLM token distributions, deterministic synchronization checks, and fail-closed decode behavior, with direct support from existing tests.

The draft also formalizes writing discipline: claims are explicitly separated into implemented facts, bounded inferences, and future-work items. This separation is central to trustworthy security writing, especially under an active experiment pause.

The next milestone is straightforward. After pause lift, we will execute the planned reproducible evaluations on recoverability, overhead, and distribution/detector-facing behavior, then update the ``almost perfect'' narrative with measured evidence under explicitly stated assumptions.
