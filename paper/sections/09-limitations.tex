\section{Current Limitations}

Ghostext currently assumes a passive channel and strict sender-receiver synchronization. This leaves active-edit robustness, paraphrase robustness, and arbitrary transport corruption outside confirmed guarantees.

The real-model path is presently covered by smoke-level integration checks rather than full benchmark-scale evaluation. Consequently, claims about naturalness, broad detector resistance, and scalability remain preliminary.

Ghostext currently uses fixed-form candidate truncation and deterministic quantization without OD-style divergence-constrained optimization~\cite{huang2026odstega}. This likely leaves utilization-security tradeoff room unexplored.

Model drift remains an operational risk. Although fingerprint checks prevent silent decode corruption, Ghostext does not yet provide a compatibility layer for cross-version recovery. In deployment terms, this means coordination overhead remains significant.

Side-channel and operational key-management concerns are also outside the current scope. The implementation uses modern authenticated encryption primitives, but broader deployment hardening (key rotation policy, audit logs, endpoint hygiene, and traffic-shape considerations) is future work.

Finally, this manuscript is intentionally conservative because of the active experiment pause. It should be read as a protocol-and-evidence baseline, not as a final empirical security claim package.
